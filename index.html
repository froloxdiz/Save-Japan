About

Our mission to revitalize Japan's forgotten spaces

The Mission

We're a group of students tackling Japan's akiya crisis head-on. With over 8.5 million abandoned houses scattered across rural Japan, we saw an opportunity to blend cultural preservation with sustainable tourism.

By transforming these forgotten structures into haunted house attractions, we're not just creating tourist destinations—we're reviving entire communities, preserving architectural heritage, and showcasing Japan's rich folklore traditions.

Why It Matters

Rural depopulation threatens Japan's cultural fabric. Our approach creates jobs, attracts international visitors, and gives new purpose to buildings that would otherwise crumble into history.

The Problem

Understanding Japan's akiya crisis

Mass Abandonment

8.5 million homes sit empty as younger generations flee to urban centers, leaving entire villages deserted.

Aging Population

Elderly owners pass away with no heirs willing to maintain ancestral properties in remote locations.

Economic Decline

Empty houses drain municipal resources and devastate local property values.

Cultural Loss

Traditional architecture and craftsmanship disappear as historic buildings decay.

Safety Hazards

Deteriorating structures pose dangers and attract illegal activities.

Village Extinction

Entire communities face disappearance as essential services close down.

The Solution

Haunted house tourism as rural revitalization

BEFORE

Abandoned structures drain community resources, lower property values, and pose safety risks while cultural heritage crumbles.

AFTER

Thriving tourist destinations generate revenue, create jobs, preserve history, and bring life back to dying towns.

Revenue Generation

Sustainable income through tourism, ticket sales, and increased local business activity.

Job Creation

Employment for actors, guides, maintenance staff, and hospitality workers.

Heritage Preservation

Restore and maintain historic architecture using traditional techniques.

International Tourism

Attract global visitors seeking authentic Japanese supernatural experiences.

Cultural Storytelling

Educate visitors about yokai folklore and traditional ghost stories.

Community Revival

Bring vitality to rural areas through increased foot traffic and services.
